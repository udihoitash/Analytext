Paper Abstract

Financial reports, including 10-K and 10-Q filings, are a primary source of textual data in business disciplines. However, extracting specific sections from these lengthy documents remains a significant challenge. When each research team develops custom code to parse these files, it results in redundancy, inefficiency, and potential inconsistencies. This is especially challenging for teams lacking technical expertise. We address these issues by offering raw textual data from MD&A, Risk Factors, and Business Description sections, alongside financial statement notes for all firms from 2008 onwards. We share Python code to facilitate the download and parsing of these filings. In addition, we also offer pre-calculated textual metrics, such as word counts, readability measures, and several bags of word metrics, including negative sentiment, forward-looking, and research and development. We contribute two new word lists, Covid and Human Capital, developed using a novel approach based on disclosure shocks. We aim to streamline research processes, ensuring consistency and enabling further advancements in the field.
